# The narcissism of small differences

The  narcissism of small differences 

is a psychological and social concept describing how groups or individuals with very similar beliefs, cultures, or identities often engage in intense conflict over minor differences. 

Coined by Sigmund Freud and later expanded by social scientists, it suggests that when two parties are closely alike, they feel a heightened need to assert their distinctiveness to protect their identity or status. 

This can lead to rivalry or hostility, as each fears being overshadowed or "replaced" by the other.
