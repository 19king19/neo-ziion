# Black History Month refers to Afro American people as a historical

Black History Month refers to Afro American people as a historical nation. 

Genetically, Afro-Americans are diverse and trace ancestry to multiple African regions, Indigenous peoples, and Europeans. 

Genetics explains biological variation, but it does not define the political, cultural, or historical category that Black History Month addresses. 

This  month exists to acknowledge a people who were systematically excluded from historical narratives despite their foundational role in building the nation.
