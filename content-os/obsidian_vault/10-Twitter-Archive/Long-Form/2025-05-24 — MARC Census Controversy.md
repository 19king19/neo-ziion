# MARC Census Controversy

MARC Census Controversy

MARC (Machine-Readable Cataloging)

What is MARC and Why It Matters?

MARC was originally a library system used to organize books. Today, it’s the logic behind how institutions store and sort identity data including the U.S. Census.

But here’s the issue:

The Problem
https://t.co/ukYtMl5ing Labels Replace Real Identity

•As the census went digital, people’s identities were boxed into preset codes.

•If you’re Afro-Latino, Afro-Arab, or multiracial, you’re often labeled as:
•“Other”
•“Two or more races”
•Or even “White” if you don’t fit neatly in a category.

2.Whiteness as Default
•The system was built to center European identity.

•So even if you check “Black,” the software might file you under “Hispanic” or “Mixed” erasing your Blackness in the data.

3.Erasure by Design
•This isn’t an accident.
•Activists and researchers have proven that Blackness gets downplayed anytime it overlaps with other identities.
