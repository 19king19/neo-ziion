# Abdulrahman Ibrahim Ibn Sori 17621829 was no ordinary enslaved man

Abdulrahman Ibrahim Ibn Sori (1762–1829) was no ordinary enslaved man. 

Born into the royal family of Fouta Djallon (modern Guinea), he was educated in Timbuktu, spoke 5 languages, and led armies. In 1788, he was captured by Mandinka raiders, traded for guns and rum, and shipped to Mississippi. His first words to his enslaver, Thomas Foster? “I am a prince. This is a mistake.” Foster laughed and called him “Prince” as a cruel joke.
