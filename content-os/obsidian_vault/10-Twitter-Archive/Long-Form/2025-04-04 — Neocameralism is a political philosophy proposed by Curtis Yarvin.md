# Neocameralism is a political philosophy proposed by Curtis Yarvin

Neocameralism is a political philosophy proposed by Curtis Yarvin (Mencius Moldbug). It reimagines modern governance as a corporate, CEO-run entity, where the government operates like a private company with shareholders, clients, and a strict focus on efficiency, order, and profitability.

Neocameralism = Sovereign State as Software Corporation

⸻

Key Tenets:

1. The State as a Company

• The nation-state functions like a joint-stock corporation.

• The CEO (or “monarch”) governs not by popular vote, but by ownership and operational efficiency.

2. Exit over Voice

• Instead of voting (voice), citizens “exit” by choosing jurisdictions or “patches” they prefer—like choosing a service provider.

3. Formalism

• All political power must be made explicit, recorded, and structured—no hidden powers or informal influence.

4. Replace Democracy with Governance Protocols

• Democracy is viewed as chaotic and corruptible.

• Governance should be deterministic, predictable, and as secure as software.

5. Patchwork Governance

• The world should consist of thousands of micro-sovereignties (“patches”) competing for residents.

• Competition drives better governance, much like capitalism does in markets.
