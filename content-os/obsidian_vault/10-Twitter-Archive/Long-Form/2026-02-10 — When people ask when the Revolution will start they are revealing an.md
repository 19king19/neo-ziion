When people ask when the Revolution will start, they are revealing an outdated understanding of how power actually moves in this era. 

They are expecting noise, confrontation, collapse, and spectacle, because those are the images of revolution they were taught by the same system they believe they are resisting. From a Keyism perspective, that expectation itself is evidence of conditioning. 

The revolution they are waiting for is a model designed by power to be visible, chaotic, and easy to neutralize.

Whoever controls how people think, what they value, and where they place their attention controls what gets built next. This is why revolutions today look quieter but last longer.
